Roll's & The Mean Value Theorem


Introduction

Roll's Theorem

Suppose that y = f(x) is continuous at every point of the closed interval[a,b] and differentiable at every point of its interior (a,b).If
f(a) = f(b).

then there is at least one number c between a and b at which
f'(c) = 0.

The Mean Value Theorem

If y=f(x) is continuous at every point of the closed interval[a,b] and differentiable at every point of its interior(a,b), then there is at least one number c between a and b at which
f'(c)(b-a) = f(b)-f(a)
